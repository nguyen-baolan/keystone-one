## The context & the Client's case

Global supply chains are under pressure to decarbonize. For agricultural buyers, particularly in coffee, cocoa, and other commodity crops, this means transforming farming practices across thousands of smallholder farms in remote regions.

Our client, a major international buyer in the Vietnamese coffee supply chain, faced this exact challenge. They had ambitious climate commitments but lacked a scalable way to support fragmented farmers in adopting regenerative, low-carbon practices.

Traditional extension services relied on periodic field visits and generic training sessions, neither scalable nor personalized enough to drive real behavior change. Farmers needed guidance tailored to their specific farm conditions, but agronomists couldn't reach every farm in person.

Off-the-shelf agricultural apps existed, but they required high digital literacy, failed to account for local farming conditions, and didn't integrate with existing buyer-farmer relationships.

---

## What we were hired to do

We were brought in to design and deploy an AI-powered platform that could deliver personalized agronomic guidance to remote farmers at scale, enabling the buyer to achieve climate goals while supporting farmer livelihoods.

## The key constraints

In this context, we faced several unique constraints:

- **Digital literacy barriers**: Most farmers had basic mobile phones and limited experience with apps
- **Fragmented farm conditions**: Each farm had unique soil, climate, crop varieties, and input practices
- **Trust and adoption**: Farmers needed to trust recommendations enough to change established practices
- **Agronomic accuracy**: AI recommendations had to match or exceed traditional extension service quality
- **Integration with existing programs**: The solution had to work within buyers' established training and certification programs
- **Connectivity challenges**: Many farms had limited or intermittent internet access

## What we did

We partnered directly with sustainability teams, agronomists, and farmer cooperatives to:

- Conduct field research to understand farmer workflows, pain points, and adoption barriers
- Design a mobile-first experience that minimized complexity: farmers simply took photos
- Build a multi-agent AI system that orchestrated agronomic analysis in the background
- Abstract complex regenerative farming practices into simple, actionable guidance
- Integrate with buyers' existing field programs for seamless rollout
- Provide agronomists with monitoring dashboards to validate AI outputs and build trust
- Deploy a pilot with select farmer groups and iterate based on real-world feedback -->

{{< accordion "Key platform capabilities:" >}}

The platform abstracted complexity through a simple user flow:

1. **Farm assessment**: Farmers photograph their crops, farm, and current fertilizers
2. **AI analysis**: Multi-agent system analyzes images, fetch farmers data from buyers' databases, finds the relevant agronomic advice bsed on the crop development stage, and identifies the next potential nudge to move farmers towards regenerative farming
3. **Personalized guidance**: Farmers receive step-by-step recommendations for transitioning to regenerative practices, based on their own farms and crop development stage
4. **Progress tracking**: Both farmers and agronomists monitor adoption and measure impact over time
5. **Monitoring**: The system generates reports that can be leveraged later for carbon verification

The AI orchestration layer handled complex agronomic reasoning:
- Crop stress and disease detection
- Derived crop development stage from a combination of data and photos
- Fertilizer optimization based on current usage and farm conditions
- Regenerative practice sequencing tailored to farm readiness
- Cost-benefit analysis for farmer decision-making

## Outcomes

Within the first 3 months, 70% of farmers enrolled in the program adopted the solution, showing that simplifying the farmers' experience was a critical factor in adoption.

### High adoption driven by ease of use and relevance

- **70% adoption rate** among pilot farmers—far exceeding typical digital agriculture uptake (15-30%)
- Farmers appreciated receiving advice tailored to their specific farms rather than generic best practices
- The photo-based interface eliminated digital literacy barriers that plagued previous initiatives

### Credible progress toward climate commitments

- The buyer gained farm-level visibility into regenerative practice adoption across their supply chain
- The platform generated auditable records suitable for ESG reporting
- Sustainability teams could track progress in real-time rather than relying on periodic audits
- The system created a foundation for outcome-based incentive programs tied to verified farm transitions

### Scalable agronomic impact without linear cost growth

- Agronomists extended their reach from dozens to hundreds of farms without proportional headcount increases
- Expert judgment was embedded in AI reasoning, enablign recommendation quality at scale
- The platform identified farms needing hands-on intervention, optimizing scarce field extension resources
- Training programs shifted from generic sessions to targeted support for specific farmer segments

### Economic benefits for farmers

- Farmers reduced input costs through optimized fertilizer usage, improving margins
- Soil health improvements positioned farms for potential long-term productivity gains
- Risk of crop failure decreased through improved agronomic monitoring and early intervention

In short, the outcome was not just a "farming app," but a **scalable climate transformation infrastructure enabled by AI**: one that aligned buyer commitments with farmer economics, enabled personalized support at scale, and created auditable progress toward measurable climate goals.
